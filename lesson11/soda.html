Spring is Near- but Winter is Sneaky! Plan Ahead and Stay Safe

by Martin Moore

In Idaho, temperatures can fluctuate wildly from one day to the next, or even from hour to hour, and Soda Springs is certainly no exception. If you‚Äôre planning any kind of outing, remember to be prepared for conditions to change. A few safety precautions can save a life, or at the very least, make you far more comfortable in an unforeseen situation.

Always carry more water than you think you will need. Never go exploring without plenty of water, even if you only expect to be on a short hike. Short hikes occasionally turn into unexpectedly long ones, and dehydration is a very real danger in any weather.

Have a warm jacket or blanket for each person in your group, even if the weather seems mild.Carry your jacket in a backpack if you don‚Äôt want to be encumbered by it. Temperatures can plummet rapidly with very little notice. Hypothermia is not fun, trust us.

Protein bars are lightweight and don‚Äôt take up too much space beside your jacket in that backpack. Trail mix, jerky, and granola bars are other popular snacks to carry. Even barring an emergency situation, having food with you can give you much-needed energy. Who can have fun when they‚Äôre hangry?

Road trips are no exception! Keep extra coats, blankets, water, and snacks in your vehicle year-round. Just because you plan to be in your car does not mean you cannot get cold, thirsty, and hungry. Thank us later. (Note: if you‚Äôre staying in an area frequented by bears, always lock your food up in a bear-proof box. No, your ice chest is not bear-proof.)

First aid kits can make a tremendous difference in how comfortable you are. Consider what items you may use regularly- like pain reliever and lotion- and other necessities like bandages, antibacterial ointment, hand wipes and instant-heat packets.

Carry a whistle everywhere you go when you are on an outing. Keep it with you. Your voice will give out a lot faster than the whistle will, and the sound won't carry as far. If you need to be found, a whistle can save your energy and your lungs while making a loud noise that can be far more easily tracked.

Stay alert! Being aware of weather conditions and your immediate surroundings can go a long way toward keeping you safe. Idaho is an amazing place, and you want to enjoy every moment of your forays. Be prepared, stay safe, and have a great time out there!
